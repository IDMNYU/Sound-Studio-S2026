**Synthesis Concepts**:

- *Oscillators*: create tones with basic wave shapes (sine wave, saw tooth, etc). A synthesizer's VCO (Voltage Controlled Oscillator) changes the pitch based on voltage.
- *LFOs*: low frequency oscillators, used to modulate other sounds
- *Filters/VCF*: A filter reduces or amplifies different frequencies, affecting the timbre of the sound
- *Amp*: Controls the volume of the sound
- *Envelope / ADSR*: Controls how the sound changes over time according to:
    - *Attack*: How fast the sound rises at the start
    - *Decay*: Time it takes for the sound to fall from its attack volume to its sustain level
    - *Sustain*: The volume level after the attack, held as long as the note is active
    - *Release*: The time it takes for the sound to fade out

- *Subtractive synthesis*: Start with a harmonically-rich waveform, like a saw or square wave, and cut the frequencies using filtering to shape a sound
- *Additive Synthesis*: Create sounds by adding sine waves together (fundamentals and partials)
- *Frequency Modulation (FM) synthesis*: Uses one waveform to modulate another
